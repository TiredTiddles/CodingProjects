Cosmic Microwave Background Radiation


This is something that is very cool. It provides evidence that the big bang happened.


What was CMBR?

According to the big bang theory at the beginning the universe was both extremely dense and hot, which didn't allow photons to travel freely, as other electrons scattered them.


Recombination occurred when the universe was 380,000 years old, and light could finally travel freely. It is this light that we call CMBR.


The unvierse isn't so hot anymore because it's been 13.8 billion years, and the universe has become much bigger, and hence cooler since then




This is an image I stole from wikipedia. Hopefully it's high quality. It depicts a temperature map of CMBR from theq Planck Spacecraft.


What did it help us discover?

This helped us discover a variety of things:

  • The age of the universe
  • A more accurate Hubble constant
  • Proportions of dark matter, dark energy, and normal matter
  • The geometry of the universe (it's almost flat)

This means that it was pretty important.
